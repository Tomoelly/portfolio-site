About

The long way round is part of the work.

I did not arrive at software through a straight hallway. That has made me more sensitive to context, people, and the real shape of problems.

I started in plant science at NTU, then worked across hotel management in Palau, HR, operations at a tea startup, and a vet clinic before becoming a backend engineer.

The detour is not a gap. It is why I care whether a feature survives real operations, why I ask how people will actually use it, and why I like building software that gives messy work a clearer shape.

These days my center of gravity is .NET backend systems, LINE integrations, AI-assisted development, and practical products.
